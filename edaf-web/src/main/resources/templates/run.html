Diversity Signals

How to read Diversity panel

Population diversity measures spread among all sampled solutions in one generation. Higher values usually mean broader exploration, lower values mean stronger concentration.

Elite diversity measures spread only among selected elite candidates. A rapid drop can indicate exploitation pressure and potential premature convergence.

Interpretation pattern: if both curves collapse early and the best fitness stalls, you likely need stronger exploration or restart settings.

Model Drift Signals

How to read Drift panel

Drift quantifies model change between consecutive generations (e.g., binary probability-vector distance, permutation consensus shift, or Gaussian KL-like change).

High drift means aggressive distribution updates; very low drift across many generations with no fitness gain can indicate stagnation.

Useful rule: combine drift with diversity. Low drift + low diversity + flat fitness is a strong stagnation signature.

How to read Insights panel

Heatmaps show probability/uncertainty structure by generation. Horizontal bands indicate dimensions/bits/items that remain stable, while strong color transitions indicate active learning.

Top Changing Bit Probabilities tracks bits with largest probability movement over time. Curves near 0 or 1 indicate fixation; oscillation around 0.5 indicates uncertainty.

Fixation Ratio Curve is the fraction of near-decided variables. Fast growth early in the run can be healthy exploitation, but if fitness does not improve it may signal premature convergence.

Permutation consensus drift shows how fast the consensus ordering changes. Falling drift with stable fitness means consolidation; high drift with poor fitness can indicate unstable model updates.

Real-valued sigma/mean views show uncertainty and center movement per dimension. Shrinking sigma with weak fitness gains usually calls for exploration boosts or restart logic.
